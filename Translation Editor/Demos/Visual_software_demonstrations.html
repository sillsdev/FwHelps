Visual software demonstrations

Bible translators need learning resources to improve their productivity, but they are usually too busy to invest much time in training. You can use demos to learn or review skills when you need them.

A software demonstration shows the procedure to perform a task. These visual demos consist of slides with explanations and visual images, similar to PowerPoint presentations. A Web browser displays each slide as a Web page.

  • You read an explanation of the procedure.

  • You see screen shots of the steps of the procedure.

  • You do the steps to click a command on a menu, a button in a dialog box, or text in a view.

    You do not do the steps to double-click, right-click, press keys, or type text.

See and read visual demos

  1. On the Help menu of the Translation Editor window, click Demos.

    A Web browser window displays the index page of hyperlinks.

    Because demo files are based on Web standards, you can view them in any browser, including Chrome, Firefox, Internet Explorer, Opera, and Safari.

    Your computer does not need to be connected to the Internet when you view demos that FieldWorks installed.

  2. To see and read a visual demo, click a hyperlink in the index page.

    The first slide displays an example, diagram, chart, or picture.

  3. Click to go at your own pace. Do any of the following:

    • To go to the next slide, click Next at the upper right of the slide.

    • To go from the last slide to the index page, click Index at the upper right of the slide.

    • If you see Next or Index in italic font style at the upper right, click where a callout points in the screen shot.

      The screen shots show only the relevant part of the Translation Editor window.

    • To go to the previous slide, click the Back button in the browser window.
