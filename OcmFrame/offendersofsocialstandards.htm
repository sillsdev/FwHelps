626.4 Offenders of Social Standards

Description

Reasons offenders break social rules (e.g., tension, stress, conflict caused by standards, etc.); treatment of offenders; attitude toward offenders; results when an offender habitually breaches social norms (e.g., they become outcasts, they become maladjusted, they become criminals, etc.); reactions of offender to judgment (e.g., repentance, indifference, rebellion, etc.)
