201.1 Non-verbal Communication

Description

Gestures or signals used when accepting or giving something (e.g., a gift, honor, etc.); gestures used to express apology or to appease anger; non-verbal expressions of emotion (e.g., sorrow, joy, happiness, offense, anger, disgust, mockery, surprise, fear, pride, etc.); non-verbal expressions of sensation (e.g., full, hungry, sleepy, hot, cold, bitter, sweet, etc); non-verbal expressions of proportion (e.g., long, short, tall, big, small, fat, etc.); expressions of greeting or farewell (e.g., waving, nodding, shaking hands, kissing, having eye contact, etc.); signals used when propositioning someone, (e.g., woman to man, man to woman, same sex); gestures used in relationships (e.g., to show respect, honor, humility, submission, etc.); gestures used in reaction to abnormal people or circumstances; etc.
