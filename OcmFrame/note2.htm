Some scholars have applied and expanded the OCM categorization system. For example, Hesung Chun Koh, applying the OCM classification to her Korean social science and humanities data, expanded the OCM codes to five-digit numeric codes beginning in the mid 1960s. The publications that include these codes are the following: Social Science Resources on Korea: A Preliminary Computerized Bibliography. New Haven: Human Relations Area Files, 2 volumes, 1968; Korea: An Analytical Guide to Bibliographies, New Haven: Human Relations Area Files, 1971; Korea, Thailand and Ceylon: Bibliography of Asian Studies, Pilot Project I. Association for Asian Studies, 1971; Korean Family and Kinship Studies Guide, New Haven: Human Relations Area Files, 1980; and Korean and Japanese Women: an Analytical Bibliographic Guide. Westport. CT: Greenwood Press, 1982. In addition, the National Museum of Ethnology (NME), a sponsoring member of HRAF, applied 5-digit OCM categories in their Japanese Cultural Information System Project under the leadership of Takao Sofue. In 1988, NME published a translation of the 5th revised edition of OCM into Japanese with the Japanese topical index including five-digit OCM subcategories (Kokuritsu Minzokugaku Hakubutsukan, honyaku. Bunka Komoku Bunrui, Osaka, National Museum of Ethnology).
