5331x Text setting

Description

This section deals with the way texts are set to music.

Some factors to consider include:

  • How are the various songs categorized? -- The GENRE may determine the text setting.
  • How different are song texts from spoken texts? -- There may be a much more repetition in song texts.
  • Who understands the song texts?

  • Where are the song texts from?

    • stories set to song
    • proverbs set to song
    • history of the ethnic group set to song
    • dreams

  • What words are used in song texts?

    • archaic form of spoken language
    • same words as used in speech
    • special song language
    • vocables
    • a mixture of the above kinds of words

  • How are the words set to music? The number of notes per speech syllable in song can be one or more of the following:

    • Syllabic: Usually one note per syllable (such as most 19th century hymns).
    • Pneumatic: Usually two to three notes per syllable.
    • Melismatic: Frequent use of one syllable sung on two or more notes.

  • Why is the music structured as it is?

    • structured to accommodate dancing during the song
    • structured to showcase the performer
    • structured to allow flexibility with words
