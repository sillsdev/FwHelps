How to use the OCM in a language-culture field project

Introduction

You can use the OCM to systematically categorize your fieldnotes and other textual material. This will make them more usable in language and culture learning, and research. The process of adding OCM categories to texts is called "tagging" or "coding."

There are many advantages to using the OCM to code texts:

  • Your data will be organized with cultural topics giving you a powerful index and retrieval system.
  • You will have a systematic way to evaluate data for depth, coverage, and critical gaps.
  • Your will be able to search your data for cultural patterns and relationships by comparing entries tagged with different OCM topics.
  • You and other researchers that use the OCM will be able to share data and develop cross-cultural comparisons.

The OCM can be used in components of a language project beyond just cataloging cultural observations.

Guidelines

When adding OCM codes to fieldnotes

  • Add OCM codes to fieldnotes as you write them.
  • Familiarize yourself with the OCM by working with major categories first. Then use the more specific subcategories.
  • Use enough codes to reference all the important topics, but not so many that the entry is filled with irrelevant information.
  • Be consistent. Check and compare entries. If more than one of you is entering and coding data, compare and evaluate your efforts so that both of you are coding data the same way.
  • Try to find a category that matches your data as closely as possible but don't hesitate to use a category that is only a partial match. An approximation is better than nothing.
  • If you need more specific codes than those already in the OCM, you can add subcategories that meet your needs.

    • Example: Ethnomusicology 5331 Music subcategories.

  • Use the OCM as a source of ideas for research topics but don’t think of it as a checklist to be completed. You will never use all the categories.

Guidelines for placing OCM tags in different media

  • Paper/Notebook options

    • Add OCM codes at the top or bottom of a field note, or
    • Add OCM codes at the edge of the page next to the pertinent data/paragraph.

  • Word processor options

    • Add OCM codes at the top or bottom of field note, or
    • Insert OCM codes as a “Comment” using the “Insert” menu, or
    • Create a two-column table and place the OCM codes in one column across from the field note text in the adjacent column.

  • The FieldWorks Data Notebook

    • See: The Outline of Cultural Material in FieldWorks.
    • For more detailed information, see: Categorizing entries in the Data Notebook user helps.

Things to do

Here are the things you can do when you use the OCM in a language-culture field project:

  • Categorize your language and culture field notes and data using OCM 's numerical codes.
  • Categorize material other than your own, such as material you plan to translate.
  • Access all data belonging to a particular category.
  • Review what you have gathered so far under any given category or set of categories that you have coded.
  • Compare other categorized material with your own for other research purposes.
  • Identify data gaps and other research possibilities.
  • Contribute to cross-cultural research by making available your categorized material.
