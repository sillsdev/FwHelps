181.1 Philosophy of Life

Description

Perceived reasons for existence; motivations for actions (e.g., why do they do what they do?); ideals both sought after and taught to children (e.g., dependence or independence, progressive or traditional, selfish or unselfish, the biggest, the bravest, the richest, etc.); fears which influence them (e.g., attitudes or actions of others, animals, occurrences such as an eclipse, spirits, death, etc.); taboos, significant things in life (e.g., sun, moon, rain, ground, spirits, etc.); perception of the future (e.g., how do they plan for the future?); etc.
