The Outline of Cultural Materials (OCM)

Description

The Outline of Cultural Materials is a manual which presents a numerical system for categorizing cultural data about all aspects of human behavior.

Also known as: 

H-RAF, the HRAF codes, or the Human Relations Area Files system

For more information, see The Human Relations Area Files (HRAF).

SIL software (Fieldworks and Lingualinks Library) uses a top-level classification system called FRAME (Framework for Research and Methodology in Ethnography) alongside the OCM in order to simplify use and access to OCM categories. All OCM categories are distributed sequentially between eleven FRAME categories.

The OCM manual has been adapted here for electronic use. A hard copy of the OCM can be obtained from HRAF, Inc. at 755 Prospect Street, New Haven, CT 06511-1225.

The OCM Manual

Here are the parts of the Outline of Cultural Materials manual:

Section Function
Preface Summarizes recent changes (2002) in the OCM
Introduction Describes the purpose, plan, theoretical assumptions, process of development, use in fieldwork, and major projects that have used the OCM for research purposes
Categories Describes the categories, gives guidelines about what information to file under a particular category, and provides cross-references to related categories
Index Provides an alphabetical list of key-words and modifiers used in the OCM with cross-references to alternative OCM categories

The OCM's numerical subject classification system

Types of categories

In the OCM there are major categories and subcategories. Both types may be used to categorize data.

There are 83 major categories which range from 100 to 910. Each major category has multiple subcategories. There are more than 700 subcategories in the OCM.

  • Major categories are represented by the first two digits in an OCM code followed by a “0”.
  • Subcategories are represented by the third, or third and forth digits of the OCM code, which replace the “0” of the major category.
