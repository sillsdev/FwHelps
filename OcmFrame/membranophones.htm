5341m Membranophones

Description

Membranophones, or skin drums, are instruments whose sound is produced by a vibrating membrane.

Membranophones are classified according to their shape.

To classify a specific membranophone, include information about other elements such as the following:

  • Whether there are one or two heads
  • If there are snares or sticky balls to give a buzz or otherwise improve the tone
  • How the skin is fixed on the drum (glued, nailed, pegged, laced, or hooped and laced)
  • Whether the drum is tuned
  • How it is tuned (loops, wedges, pedal or rods)
  • How it is played (with hands, sticks, beaters, or indirectly with striking balls)
  • Position of the drum when played
  • What the body of the drum is made of (wood, metal, earthenware/ceramics)
